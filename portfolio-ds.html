Data Science Projects

Software Requirement Specifications (SRS) Classification

Objective: Automate classification of SRS documents (type, priority, severity) to reduce manual effort and improve consistency in the SDLC.
Approach: Compare traditional deep learning (LSTM/BiLSTM with GloVe/ELMo) against transfer learning with DistilBERT; fine‑tune on labeled SRS datasets with stratified train/val/test splits.
Methods / Models: DistilBERT transformer fine‑tuning; LSTM/BiLSTM baselines; AdamW optimizer; sequence truncation/padding; weighted F1 for imbalanced classes.
Data: Large industrial SRS corpus (~83k requirements) and PROMISE‑NFR benchmark (626 requirements).
Results / Impact: DistilBERT achieved ~80% F1 for Type and ~71% for Severity, outperforming LSTM/BiLSTM; improved consistency and reduced manual classification overhead.

Probabilistic Forecasting for Predicting City‑wide Electricity Consumption

Objective: Develop accurate probabilistic forecasts of city‑level electricity demand with calibrated uncertainty for planning and policy decisions.
Approach: Use global forecasting models across multiple cities’ hourly series; compare statistical, ML, RNN, and probabilistic deep learning methods; evaluate both point accuracy and interval calibration.
Methods / Models: Baselines: Naive, SARIMAX; ML: Random Forest, Gradient Boosting; Deep Learning: LSTM, GRU; Probabilistic: DeepAR, DeepState; metrics: NRMSE/SMAPE for point, weighted pinball loss for quantiles.
Data: 81 cities’ hourly electricity consumption (~34k observations each) with calendar/time covariates (hour, weekday, month, seasonal encodings).
Results / Impact: GRU/LSTM led on point accuracy; DeepAR/DeepState produced better‑calibrated intervals at longer horizons; demonstrated value of uncertainty‑aware global models for demand planning.

Store Performance Analysis

Objective: Identify key drivers of sales and traffic across ~89 locations over multiple years.
Approach: Conduct correlation and feature‑importance analysis on operational/demographic metrics; compare year‑over‑year trends and KPIs.
Methods / Models: Correlation matrices and pairwise plots; tree‑based feature importance; descriptive analytics and BI dashboards.
Data: Sales, traffic, store size, financing %, compliance, city population (2019–2022).
Results / Impact: Traffic was the strongest predictor (~80% correlation); store size moderate (~50%); higher financing % negatively associated with sales; actionable guidance for traffic growth and footprint optimization.

Competitors’ Location Analysis

Objective: Quantify how competitor proximity and co‑tenancy influence store traffic and sales.
Approach: Compare co‑tenant vs. same‑site vs. nearby competitors; analyze distance‑based relationships and regional variation.
Methods / Models: Correlation analysis; geospatial proximity features; per‑capita normalization of traffic and sales.
Data: Store traffic/sales metrics with competitor presence categorized by proximity (co‑tenant, same‑site, nearby, distant).
Results / Impact: Co‑tenancy with major competitors showed positive impacts; certain nearby competitors boosted sales; greater distance from others correlated with stronger performance—informing site‑selection strategy.

Truck Optimization Simulation (Romeoville Case)

Objective: Optimize truck routing and resource allocation for deliveries in the Romeoville region, reducing cost and improving efficiency.
Approach: Two‑stage workflow: (1) cluster delivery points into service zones; (2) solve transportation optimization with mathematical programming; evaluate solver setups and scenarios.
Methods / Models: Clustering (K‑means style); linear programming transportation models minimizing distance/cost; solvers: IBM CPLEX and PuLP‑CPLEX; scenario‑based simulation for cost and load balance.
Data: Romeoville delivery location datasets including a Dec 20, 2020 case; variables: trucks, delivery points, demand, distance matrices.
Results / Impact: Clustering simplified the problem and improved solver performance; optimal plans minimized cost and balanced loads; scenario analysis showed measurable savings and utilization gains.

Automated Test Scoring (LLM‑based)

Objective: Build accurate, rubric‑aligned automated scoring for open‑ended responses using modern Large Language Models.
Approach: Operationalize LLM‑based scoring in Dify with iterative prompt engineering, rubric conditioning, and systematic evaluation; integrate into existing assessment platforms.
Methods / Models: LLMs: GPT‑4o, Claude, Mistral, LLaMA; techniques: few‑shot chain‑of‑thought prompts, rubric decomposition, response normalization, routing/caching for latency/cost; feature selection via Genetic Algorithm for rubric‑aligned optimization.

Results / Impact: High agreement with human raters and reduced turnaround time; improved scoring consistency; rapid rubric updates and smooth platform integration.

Food Inspections and Violations

Objective: Predict establishments at higher risk of violations to prioritize inspections and improve public‑health outcomes.
Approach: Perform EDA and cleaning; expand pipe‑separated violation codes; engineer time fields; address class imbalance; train and compare classifiers; evaluate with precision/recall/F1.
Methods / Models: One‑hot encoding for violations, facility and inspection types; feature selection via RandomForest importance; resampling (RandomUnder/OverSampler, NearMiss, SMOTE); models: GaussianNB, Decision Tree, baseline neural network; threshold tuning and classification reports.
Data: Chicago Food Inspections & Violations dataset (>100k records after cleaning); target risk levels (High/Medium/Low).
Results / Impact: Resampling boosted recall for high‑risk cases; violation indicators and inspection‑type features were strong predictors; enabled more efficient inspection allocation.

Marketing Ad Spend Analysis

Objective: Quantify channel effectiveness and ROI across categories and funnel stages, and optimize budget allocation.
Approach: Combine classification of conversion impact with regression of sales vs. weekly spend; segment by category and funnel position; compare models via cross‑validation.
Methods / Models: Classification: Logistic Regression, KNN, Decision Tree, Random Forest, SVM. Regression: Linear, Ridge, Lasso, KNN, Decision Tree, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting; tree‑based feature importance to interpret drivers.
Data: Weekly spend and sales data, segmented by product category and funnel position; features include spend levels, funnel stage, conversions.
Results / Impact: Tree‑based models (RF/GBR) outperformed linear baselines; segmentation by funnel and category significantly affected effectiveness; recommendations shifted budget to high‑ROI channels.

Predicting GO Rail Ridership

Objective: Forecast short‑ to mid‑term ridership to inform schedule, capacity, and resource planning.
Approach: End‑to‑end preprocessing from raw Excel to clean series at corridor/station/time‑period; benchmark statistical vs. ML vs. Prophet models; rolling origin evaluation and Prophet CV.
Methods / Models: Diagnostics (ADF, seasonal decomposition, ACF/PACF); ARIMA/ETS baselines; Gradient Boosting with exogenous regressors; Prophet with extra regressors (e.g., total trips, calendar effects); metrics: RMSE/MAE/R² and Prophet CV metrics.
Data: Metrolinx ridership and service data aggregated to daily frequency with calendar features and exogenous regressors; corridor/station/time‑period granularities.
Results / Impact: Prophet+regressors provided well‑calibrated forecasts and tight intervals; ML was competitive on point error; reusable pipeline supports rapid planning scenarios.

CIHI P‑Card Analytics: Clustering & Anomaly Detection

Objective: Analyze corporate purchasing‑card (P‑Card) transactions to surface spending patterns by service area, detect misclassification, and flag anomalous activity.
Approach: Load and clean multi‑year files; standardize vendor/category fields; build summary views by service area; explore spending behavior; apply unsupervised methods for clustering and anomalies.
Methods / Models: Feature scaling (StandardScaler); K‑Means clustering for spend segmentation; IsolationForest for anomaly detection; rule/keyword checks for misclassification; interactive visualizations (Plotly, seaborn).
Data: P‑Card transactions (2014–2018) with amounts, merchant/category details, and service areas; consolidated into a harmonized schema.
Results / Impact: Flagged outliers and probable misclassifications; segmented spend profiles; recommended vendor/category normalization and automated anomaly screening to strengthen oversight.

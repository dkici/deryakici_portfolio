About Me

I am Dr. Derya Kici, an interdisciplinary researcher and educator with a distinctive career at the intersection of data science, information science, educational technology, and the arts. Trained as a scholar at the University of Toronto, where I completed my Ph.D. in Curriculum Studies and Knowledge Media Design, my work bridges human-centered learning sciences with computational and AI-driven approaches to understanding information, behavior, and complex learning processes.

I currently work as a Senior IT Data Scientist at ETS Canada, developing advanced facial recognition technologies, test security analytics, and machine learning systems for high-stakes assessments. Before ETS, I served as a Data Scientist at The Dufresne Group and worked as a postdoctoral researcher at the Data Science Lab, Toronto Metropolitan University, collaborating with partners such as LG Science Park and IBM CAS Canada on deep learning, probabilistic forecasting, and NLP projects.

Alongside my industry work, I have been an instructor since 2009, teaching data analytics, AI, Python programming, big data, and instructional technologies at multiple institutions including Toronto Metropolitan University. My academic background blends learning sciences, knowledge building, information analysis, and curriculum design, supported by certifications in Artificial Intelligence and Agile Project Management.

I also bring a creative dimension to my work as a Drama Leader and Creative Drama educator, having led numerous workshops and training sessions that foster reflection, identity development, collaboration, and expressive learning processes. This artistic practice deeply informs my approach to design thinking, user experience, and human–AI interaction.
